Cheese is made out of milk, usually from cows, goats, or sheep. The milk is first acidified using a bacterial culture or an acidic substance like vinegar or lemon juice. This causes the milk proteins to coagulate and form curds. The curds are then separated from the liquid, called whey, and processed further to create different types of cheese. Cheese can be made with raw or pasteurized milk and can be flavored with various herbs, spices, or other ingredients. Different types of cheese can also be made with different types of bacterial cultures or by using different processing methods, resulting in a wide variety of textures and flavors!
